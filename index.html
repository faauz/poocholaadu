About Our Village

Our History

Poocholamadu was established in the early 18th century by settlers attracted to its fertile land and abundant water sources. The name "Poocholamadu" comes from the lush greenery that blankets the area throughout most of the year.

With a current population of approximately 2,500 residents, our village maintains a rich cultural heritage with annual festivals that celebrate our agricultural roots and diverse community.

The village is known for its harmonious coexistence of different religious and cultural groups, with temples, mosques, and churches standing side by side.

Tourist and Visitor Guide

How to Reach Poocholamadu

Our village is easily accessible by various modes of transportation:

  • By Road: Well-connected by state highways with regular bus services from the nearest city (45 km away).
  • By Rail: The nearest railway station is Valley Junction (15 km away) with taxis available.
  • By Air: The closest airport is City International Airport (80 km away) with car rental options.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Poocholamadu is between October and March when the weather is pleasant. The annual harvest festival in January is a particularly vibrant time to experience our culture.

Local Customs

Visitors are welcomed warmly in our village. We appreciate when guests:

  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Ask permission before photographing locals
  • Participate respectfully in cultural events
  • Try to learn a few words in our local dialect

Places to Stay

While we don't have large hotels, visitors can enjoy authentic experiences:

  • Village Homestays: Live with local families (₹500-1000 per night)
  • Guest House: Basic but clean accommodations (₹800 per night)
  • Nearby Resorts: Luxury options within 20 km drive
